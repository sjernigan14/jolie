French Bistro Classics Meet North Carolina

July 10, 2025

Classical French cooking is built on technique — the mother sauces, the braises, the way a proper roast chicken should look and taste. But technique without great ingredients is just mechanics. The ingredient is the point. The technique is how you honor it.

That's the philosophy at Jolie. Our chef sources from farms and producers across North Carolina — beef from the Piedmont, seafood from the coast, seasonal vegetables that change the menu as the year turns. When a dish like duck cassoulet or steak frites au poivre shows up on your table, it carries both traditions: the French technique that gives it structure, and the North Carolina ingredients that give it soul.

We don't make a big deal about it on the menu. You won't see farm names listed next to every ingredient or a manifesto about sourcing on the back page. It's just how we cook. The relationships with our farmers and fishmongers have been built over years, and they're the reason the food tastes the way it does.

The MICHELIN Guide recognized Jolie in its 2025 selection — a distinction that belongs to the entire team, from the line cooks who execute every plate to the servers who deliver it with the kind of care that makes people come back. But the foundation of all of it is the same thing it's always been: exceptional ingredients, classical technique, and the belief that every dish should earn its place on the menu.
