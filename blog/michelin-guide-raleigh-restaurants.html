MICHELIN Guide Restaurants in Raleigh: What It Means to Dine at Jolie

December 18, 2024

When MICHELIN expanded into North Carolina, Raleigh's dining scene got the kind of outside validation that locals already knew was overdue. Jolie was among the restaurants recognized — a French bistro on Person Street in Historic Oakwood, built by James Beard Award semifinalist Chef Scott Crawford. Here's what that recognition actually means when you sit down to eat.

What MICHELIN Recognition Means (and Doesn't)

The MICHELIN Guide is the most recognized restaurant rating system in the world. Inspectors eat anonymously, pay their own bills, and evaluate on five criteria: quality of ingredients, mastery of cooking techniques, the personality of the chef in the cuisine, value for money, and consistency. A listing in the Guide means inspectors visited, ate, and determined the restaurant met their standard.

What it doesn't mean: white tablecloths, a dress code, or a bill that requires a second mortgage. Jolie is a bistro. The atmosphere is warm and lively — not hushed. You'll hear conversation, laughter, the sound of a working kitchen. The food is technically excellent because it has to be, but the experience is built for enjoyment, not intimidation.

What to Expect at Jolie

The menu is French bistro with modern adaptations. The escargot is textbook. The steak frites — dry-aged, seared hard, served with hand-cut frites and a green peppercorn sauce — is the dish most tables order. The duck cassoulet is slow-braised and deeply seasoned. The steak tartare is prepared tableside. French onion soup arrives with a cap of broiled Gruyère that you have to crack through. These aren't dishes designed to surprise you with novelty. They're designed to be the best version of themselves.

The wine list leans French, organized by region, with enough by-the-glass options that you can drink well without committing to a full bottle. The bar program — led by Beverage Director Jordan Joseph — is equally considered, with classic cocktails and seasonal builds that complement the food.

The Spaces

Jolie has several distinct dining experiences under one roof. The main dining room is the heart of the restaurant — white brick, brass shelving, warm lighting. Six counter seats overlook the open kitchen, which is the best seat in the house if you want to watch the line work. Le Rooftop offers thirty outdoor seats with string lights and the Raleigh skyline. And beneath the restaurant, Sous Terre is a private underground cocktail club that Jolie diners access for free.

Who's Behind It

Chef Scott Crawford is a James Beard Award semifinalist who's been cooking in the Triangle for over two decades. Jolie is named for his daughter and inspired by a family trip to Paris — the bistros, the markets, the conviction that great food should feel welcoming, not exclusive. He also operates Crawford Brothers Steakhouse in Cary, Brodeto (Adriatic seafood), and Crawford & Son on Person Street.

How to Book

Reservations are on Tock. Friday and Saturday fill first, especially rooftop seats when the weather is good. Midweek — Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday — is easier to get and just as good. The kitchen doesn't scale back on a Wednesday.

Make a reservation at Jolie and see what a MICHELIN Guide restaurant in Raleigh actually feels like. It feels like a neighborhood bistro that happens to be excellent.
