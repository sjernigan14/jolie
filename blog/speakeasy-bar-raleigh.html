Sous Terre: Raleigh's Underground Speakeasy Beneath Jolie

March 15, 2026

There's a door downstairs at Jolie that most people walk right past. No sign, no velvet rope, no bouncer with a clipboard. Just a door, a key, and whatever's happening on the other side. That's Sous Terre — Chef Scott Crawford's underground cocktail club on Person Street in Raleigh.

What Is Sous Terre?

The name means "underground" in French, and the description is literal. Sous Terre sits directly beneath Jolie, accessible by a staircase that drops you into a low-lit room with exposed brick, dark wood, and the kind of quiet that makes conversation feel more interesting. It's a cocktail club — not a bar, not a lounge, not a speakeasy in the Instagram sense of the word. There's no password. There's a key.

Membership gets you that key. But if you're dining at Jolie, you get complimentary access for the evening. Finish your steak frites, settle the check, and ask your server about heading downstairs. That's it. No cover, no reservation for the bar — just walk down.

The Cocktails

Beverage Director Jordan Joseph runs the program at Sous Terre, and the cocktail list reflects someone who takes the craft seriously without taking themselves too seriously. The menu rotates seasonally — you'll find classic builds executed with precision alongside original drinks that pull from French and Southern ingredients. Think calvados old fashioneds, lavender-honey gimlets, and a Champagne cocktail that changes with whatever Jordan's excited about that week.

The wine list is tight and intentional. Mostly French, mostly by the glass, built to complement what you just ate upstairs or to stand on its own. There's no bottle service, no VIP table. The room is the experience.

The Space

Sous Terre seats around 40, and it fills up — especially on Friday and Saturday nights after the dining room clears. The design is moody without being precious: dark walls, candlelight, a bar that feels like it's been there longer than the building. The acoustics are deliberately intimate. You can actually hear the person across from you, which in 2026 qualifies as a luxury.

It's the kind of room where a nightcap turns into three, where a first date becomes a third, where you and your friends end up closing the place because nobody checked the time.

Why It Works After Dinner at Jolie

Most Raleigh restaurants end the evening at the check. You sign, you leave, you figure out where to go next. At Jolie, the evening extends. You've just had escargot and duck cassoulet and a bottle of Cru Beaujolais in a MICHELIN-recognized dining room — and now you can walk downstairs to a private cocktail club without getting in a car. The transition feels natural, like the second act of the same night.

This is especially useful for celebrations, rehearsal dinners, or any evening where you want the night to keep going without losing the atmosphere. Sous Terre isn't louder or more chaotic than dinner. It's just different — quieter, darker, more focused on the drink in your hand.

Getting There

Sous Terre is located beneath Jolie at 620 N. Person Street in Historic Oakwood, Raleigh — the same stretch of Person Street that's home to Crawford & Son. Chef Crawford's other restaurants include Crawford Brothers Steakhouse in Cary and Brodeto (Adriatic seafood), but Sous Terre is the one you can only reach through Jolie's basement. If you're dining upstairs, just ask your server. If you're a member, you already know the way.

For everyone else: book a table at Jolie, enjoy dinner, and let the evening take you downstairs. It's the best hidden bar in Raleigh — and you don't even have to find it. It finds you.
