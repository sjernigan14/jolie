Jolie: A French Bistro Minutes from Downtown Raleigh

March 15, 2026

Downtown Raleigh has more restaurants than it knows what to do with. What it has less of is a neighborhood bistro where the food is MICHELIN Guide-recognized, the rooftop seats thirty, and there's a private cocktail club in the basement. Jolie is that restaurant — five minutes from the heart of downtown, on Person Street in Historic Oakwood.

The Location

620 N. Person Street sits at the edge of Historic Oakwood, one of Raleigh's oldest and most walkable neighborhoods. It's close enough to downtown that you can be here from Fayetteville Street in under ten minutes, but far enough that the street has its own character — Victorian homes, old oaks, sidewalks that actually get used. The restaurant shares Person Street with Crawford & Son (Chef Crawford's American concept next door) and Bar Sous Terre, and is a short walk from several of Oakwood's best blocks. Crawford's other restaurants — Crawford Brothers Steakhouse in Cary and Brodeto (Adriatic seafood) — are elsewhere in the Triangle, but Person Street is where it all started.

If you're coming from the Convention Center, the Warehouse District, Glenwood South, or any downtown hotel, Jolie is a quick rideshare or a manageable drive. Street parking on Person and Pace Streets is free in the evenings, and the 800 N. Person St parking lot is a four-minute walk.

What Makes Jolie Different

Most restaurants near downtown compete on proximity. Jolie competes on the food. Chef Scott Crawford — a James Beard Award semifinalist — built the menu around French bistro classics executed at a level that earned MICHELIN Guide recognition. The escargot is the real thing. The steak frites uses dry-aged beef with hand-cut frites and green peppercorn sauce. The duck cassoulet is braised for hours. The French onion soup has a Gruyère cap that shatters when you break through it.

The wine list is French-forward, organized by region, with enough by-the-glass selections to drink well without committing to a bottle. The cocktail program, led by Beverage Director Jordan Joseph, pulls from French and Southern influences.

Three Experiences in One Address

Jolie isn't just a dining room. Le Rooftop adds thirty outdoor seats with string lights and skyline views — one of the few true rooftop dining options near downtown Raleigh. Six counter seats face the open kitchen, letting you watch the brigade work through service. And beneath the restaurant, Sous Terre is a private underground cocktail club that Jolie diners can access for free after their meal.

That range — a proper dining room, a rooftop, a kitchen counter, and a basement bar — means you can come to the same address for completely different evenings. A quiet Tuesday dinner at the counter. A Friday rooftop date. A Saturday group dinner followed by cocktails underground.

Nearby

Jolie is also well-positioned for evenings that include a show. Red Hat Amphitheater, Memorial Auditorium, and Fletcher Opera Theater are all within a seven-minute drive. Pre-concert dinner at Jolie is one of the best moves in downtown Raleigh — book a 5:30, eat well, arrive at the venue relaxed.

Book It

Open Tuesday through Saturday, 5 PM onward. Reservations on Tock. Midweek is easiest to book; weekends fill fast, especially rooftop seats in spring and fall.
