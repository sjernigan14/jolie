Girls' Night Out at Jolie: French Dinner, Rooftop, and a Secret Bar

March 15, 2026

The group chat has been going for three weeks. Everyone's busy. Someone suggests next Thursday. Someone else can't do Thursday. Finally, a date lands. Now you need a place that's actually worth the coordination — somewhere with good food, good drinks, and a vibe that isn't a sports bar with flatscreen TVs. Jolie is built for exactly this night.

Start with the Food

Jolie's menu is French bistro — the kind of food that's meant to be shared and talked over. Start the table with escargot, the charcuterie board, and the steak tartare (prepared tableside, which is a conversation in itself). Order a bottle of Sancerre or Cru Beaujolais from the French-leaning wine list and let the evening build from there.

For mains, the steak frites is the signature — dry-aged, hand-cut frites, green peppercorn sauce. The duck cassoulet is rich and shareable. The roast chicken is the kind of dish that makes someone at the table say "why can't I make chicken like this at home." Save room for dessert, because the pastry program is serious.

Take It to the Rooftop

If the weather's right, start or finish on Le Rooftop. Thirty seats, string lights, open sky, and the kind of atmosphere that makes everyone put their phones down (or at least use them for group photos). It's the most photogenic dining spot in Raleigh, and it's intimate enough that your group isn't lost in a sea of other tables.

Ask about the seasonal cocktails up top — Beverage Director Jordan Joseph keeps the rooftop menu tight and interesting.

Then Go Underground

Here's the part that makes Jolie different from every other girls' night option in Raleigh. After dinner, ask your server about Sous Terre — Chef Scott Crawford's underground cocktail club directly beneath the restaurant. Jolie diners get complimentary access. No cover, no separate reservation. You just walk downstairs.

Sous Terre is dark, candlelit, and built around serious cocktails. The vibe shifts from lively bistro to low-key lounge without anyone getting in a car or waiting in a line. One round of nightcaps down there and the evening goes from a good dinner to a full experience.

Why It Works for Groups

Jolie handles group dining well because the space is designed for it. Tables can be configured for parties of four to fourteen. The kitchen paces courses for the whole table, not piecemeal. And the price point — entrées in the $30-45 range, shareable starters, wines by the glass — lands in that sweet spot where it feels special without anyone doing math in their head.

Book early for Friday and Saturday. Midweek (Tuesday through Thursday) is easier to get and just as lively — the full menu runs every night.

Reserve your table on Tock. Tell the group chat it's handled.
