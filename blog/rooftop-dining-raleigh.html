Le Rooftop: Raleigh's Best-Kept Secret for Outdoor Dining

February 28, 2026

If you haven't been up to Le Rooftop yet, you're missing one of the best dining experiences in the Triangle — and we say that knowing full well we're biased.

It's just one flight up from the main dining room, but it feels like a different place entirely. The canopy overhead was designed by Louis Cherry Architecture to give you shelter without walls — open enough to feel the breeze, covered enough to keep the evening intimate. Climbing greenery frames every corner, string lights set the mood, and the whole thing was calibrated so it feels like dusk all night long.

The menu is the same one you'd find downstairs — steak frites, escargot, roast chicken, the full wine list and craft cocktail program. The service carries the same warmth. The only difference is that you're eating under the sky, with a view of Historic Oakwood, and the kind of evening air that makes you order one more glass of wine.

We're open seasonally, with heat lamps and covered areas that extend the season further than you'd expect. Some of the best nights up there are in early fall — crisp air, warm food, nowhere else to be.

Le Rooftop can also be reserved exclusively for private events — dinners, celebrations, corporate outings. It's become one of our most requested spaces, and once you've been up there, you'll understand why.
