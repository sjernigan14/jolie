Mother's Day at Jolie: The Brunch She Actually Wants

March 15, 2026

Every year, the same scramble. Where are we taking Mom? Somewhere nice, but not stuffy. Somewhere with good food, but not a prix fixe that costs more than the gift. Somewhere she'll actually enjoy — not just tolerate while the kids spill orange juice. Jolie is that place.

Why French Bistro Works for Mother's Day

French bistro food is comfort food with technique behind it. The flavors are familiar enough that everyone at the table finds something they love, but refined enough that it feels like an occasion. Think buttery croissants, perfectly poached eggs, steak frites that make her forget she cooked dinner for the family 300 nights last year. This is a meal that says we thought about it.

Jolie's kitchen runs under the same standards that earned a spot in the MICHELIN Guide — and that quality shows up whether it's a Tuesday night or the busiest brunch day of the year. Chef Scott Crawford's team doesn't phone in holidays. They lean into them.

Le Rooftop on a May Morning

If the weather cooperates — and in Raleigh in May, it usually does — ask about Le Rooftop. Thirty seats, string lights, open air, and the kind of calm that makes a Sunday morning feel like a vacation. It's intimate enough for a small family celebration and pretty enough that she'll want photos. Let her have the moment.

The Counter Seats

For the mom who actually loves food — the one who watches cooking shows, who has opinions about butter — the six counter seats overlooking the open kitchen are the move. She'll watch the line work up close: eggs being finished, sauces being built, pastry coming out of the oven. It's the most engaging seat in the house, and it shows her you know what she's into.

Keep It Simple to Book

Mother's Day reservations at Jolie fill fast. This is not the weekend to wing it. Book on Tock as early as you can — specify rooftop if you want outdoor seating. Arrive a few minutes early so she's not rushing. Park on Person or Pace Street, or use the 800 N. Person St lot (four-minute walk through Historic Oakwood, which is beautiful in spring).

After Brunch

Walk the Oakwood neighborhood. It's one of Raleigh's oldest residential districts — Victorian homes, massive oaks, sidewalks that feel like a different city. Or stop by the bar for a glass of Champagne before heading home. If you're celebrating in the evening instead, ask your server about heading down to Sous Terre after dinner — Jolie diners get complimentary access to Chef Crawford's underground cocktail club.

She doesn't need another candle. She needs a really good meal and two hours where nobody asks her for anything. Reserve your table. And sign up for our email list — holidays like Mother's Day, Valentine's Day, and New Year's Eve get special menus announced to subscribers first.
