Date Night at Jolie: Three Ways to Make It Count

March 15, 2026

A good date night has a few non-negotiables: the food has to be worth the reservation, the atmosphere can't feel generic, and you need to be able to actually hear each other. Jolie covers all three — and then adds a rooftop and a secret bar for good measure.

Option One: The Rooftop

Le Rooftop is thirty seats on the second floor of Jolie, open-air with string lights and the Raleigh skyline in the distance. It's the most intimate outdoor dining in Raleigh — small enough that it never feels crowded, scenic enough that you don't need to manufacture atmosphere. Spring through fall, this is the move. Request rooftop seating when you book on Tock, and arrive a few minutes early to settle in.

Option Two: The Kitchen Counter

Six seats overlooking the open kitchen. You'll watch Chef Crawford's team work through service — searing steaks, finishing sauces, plating dishes that go directly to the dining room. It's the most engaging seat in the house and the best conversation starter if you're early in the relationship or late enough in it that you need a new one. Ask for counter seats when booking.

Option Three: The Full Arc

The best date night at Jolie uses all three floors. Start in the dining room or on the rooftop. Order the escargot and the steak tartare (prepared tableside — this alone is worth the reservation). Share the steak frites. Split a bottle of Burgundy. Let the kitchen pace the meal so it doesn't feel rushed.

Then, instead of walking to your car and going home, ask your server about Sous Terre. Chef Crawford's underground cocktail club is directly beneath the restaurant, and Jolie diners get complimentary access. Walk downstairs into a candlelit room with exposed brick, serious cocktails, and the kind of quiet that makes you want to stay for one more round. That's where the date goes from good dinner to full evening.

The Details That Matter

The wine list is French-forward and well-priced by the glass. If you're not sure what to order, tell your server what you like and let them guide you — Beverage Director Jordan Joseph trains the team well. The cocktails are equally considered, with seasonal builds that change throughout the year.

Jolie is at 620 N. Person Street in Historic Oakwood — one of Raleigh's prettiest neighborhoods, especially for a walk before or after dinner. Street parking on Person and Pace Streets. The 800 N. Person St lot is a four-minute walk if you need it.

When to Go

Friday and Saturday are the obvious date nights, and they book up. But Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday at Jolie are just as good — same full menu, slightly easier to get your preferred time, and the midweek crowd tends to be more relaxed. The kitchen doesn't hold anything back on a Tuesday.

Book on Tock. Specify rooftop or counter if you have a preference. Show up with nothing else planned — the evening will figure itself out.
