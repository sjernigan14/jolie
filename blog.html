A Taste of Paris on Person Street

Jolie was born from a cherished family memory and a dream to bring authentic French bistro culture to Raleigh. Years ago, our family took a transformative trip to Paris, wandering through charming neighborhoods, savoring simple yet elegant meals, and discovering the true meaning of joie de vivre. That experience inspired us to create a space where locals could experience that same magic right here in Historic Oakwood. Our daughter, Jolie, became the namesake of this restaurant—a tribute to her radiant spirit and the joy we wanted to share with every guest who walks through our doors.

French Bistro Classics Meet North Carolina

At Jolie, we honor traditional French culinary techniques while celebrating the incredible local ingredients that North Carolina has to offer. Our chef sources from nearby farms and producers, ensuring that every plate reflects both authentic French tradition and regional pride. From locally sourced beef and fresh seafood to seasonal vegetables that showcase the best of the Piedmont, we believe that great cuisine is about respecting ingredients and technique equally. This commitment to farm-to-table dining hasn't gone unnoticed—our dedication to excellence has earned us recognition in the MICHELIN Guide, a testament to our team's passion and precision in the kitchen.

A Family Table: The Crawford Hospitality Story

Jolie is part of a larger tapestry of exceptional dining experiences crafted by the Crawford Hospitality family. From the elegant simplicity of Crawford & Son and the rustic elegance of Brodeto, to the classic steakhouse tradition of Crawford Brothers and the intimate ambiance of Bar Sousterre, our collective vision celebrates the art of hospitality in all its forms. Each concept is distinct, yet all are united by our commitment to culinary excellence, impeccable service, and creating spaces where memories are made. We invite you to explore our sister restaurants and discover the unique story each one tells. Together, we're redefining dining in Raleigh, one table at a time.
