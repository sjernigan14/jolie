5 Reasons Jolie Is the Perfect Rehearsal Dinner Venue

We've hosted enough rehearsal dinners to know what makes them work — and what makes them feel like just another group dinner at a restaurant. The difference is usually in the details.

Le Rooftop changes the energy of the evening. There's something about being outdoors, one flight up from Person Street, with candles on the tables and the Oakwood sky overhead, that loosens people up. The toasts feel more natural. The laughter carries.

The menu can be whatever you want it to be — a multi-course seated dinner built around the steak frites and roast chicken, or family-style platters of escargot, charcuterie, and duck cassoulet. We're five minutes from the downtown hotels, the room does the decorating for you, and our events team handles the rest.

Planning a Private Event at Jolie

We've done birthday dinners for twelve and cocktail receptions for a hundred and twenty. Every event is different, but the approach is the same — we listen to what you want, then we make it happen.

The space is flexible — reserve Le Rooftop exclusively, book a section of the dining room, or buy out the entire restaurant. The food is the centerpiece: our executive chef will design a custom menu around whatever you're envisioning, with the same seasonal ingredients and classical French technique we serve on a regular Tuesday night.

If you're thinking about it, reach out. No pressure, no minimum spend surprises — just a conversation about what you need.

February 28, 2026

Le Rooftop: Raleigh's Best-Kept Secret for Outdoor Dining

If you haven't been up to Le Rooftop yet, you're missing one of the best dining experiences in the Triangle — and we say that knowing full well we're biased.

Just one flight up from the main dining room, but it feels like a different place entirely. The canopy by Louis Cherry Architecture gives you shelter without walls. Climbing greenery frames every corner. The menu is the same one you'd find downstairs — steak frites, escargot, the full wine list. The only difference is that you're eating under the sky.

Some of the best nights up there are in early fall — crisp air, warm food, nowhere else to be.

November 15, 2025

A Taste of Paris on Person Street

A few years ago, our family took a trip to Paris. We watched our daughter Jolie fall completely in love with it — the language, the food, the general joie de vivre. That trip stayed with us. When it came time to open a second restaurant, the concept was obvious.

Jolie opened in August 2019 at 620 N. Person Street. Within hours of opening reservations, nearly 2,000 tables were booked. That kind of response doesn't come from marketing — it comes from a community that was ready for this kind of place. Five years in, the feeling hasn't changed.

September 20, 2025

Dining Under the Stars on Le Rooftop

There's a version of a perfect Raleigh evening that goes like this: warm air, a table set with candlelight, the quiet hum of conversation, and the full Jolie menu at your fingertips. That's Le Rooftop on any given Tuesday through Saturday.

The space was designed by Louis Cherry Architecture. The custom canopy provides shelter without enclosure. Wrought-iron details nod to the Parisian terraces that inspired Jolie in the first place. And the lighting was set so it feels like golden hour all night long. Some nights up there feel like they shouldn't end. That's by design.

July 10, 2025

French Bistro Classics Meet North Carolina

Classical French cooking is built on technique — the mother sauces, the braises, the way a proper roast chicken should look and taste. But technique without great ingredients is just mechanics. The ingredient is the point. The technique is how you honor it.

Our chef sources from farms across North Carolina — beef from the Piedmont, seafood from the coast, seasonal vegetables that change the menu as the year turns. We don't list farm names on the menu or write a manifesto about sourcing. It's just how we cook. The MICHELIN Guide recognized Jolie in its 2025 selection — a distinction that belongs to the entire team.

Hosting Your Celebration at Jolie

Some of the best nights we've had at Jolie weren't on the calendar. The birthday that turned into the whole table singing. The proposal where the kitchen timed the ring to arrive with dessert. The anniversary at the same table from their first date.

We love these nights. And we've gotten good at making them happen. For smaller celebrations, our team can set aside a section of the dining room. For bigger events, Le Rooftop is available for exclusive buyouts. The food makes it easy — our kitchen will build a custom menu around your evening.

March 15, 2025

The Art of the French Wine List

Our wine list is short on purpose. Every bottle on it earned its place — either because it pairs beautifully with something on the menu, or because it's the kind of discovery that makes you want to learn more about a region you hadn't thought much about before. Probably both.

The list leans French, but it's not a textbook. You'll find Burgundies alongside bottles from the Jura and producers in the Languedoc who punch well above their appellation. The cocktail program follows the same philosophy — a proper Kir, a well-made Sidecar, seasonal creations from our bar team. If you're the kind of person who likes to linger over a glass after the plates are cleared, you'll feel at home here.

January 20, 2025

A Family Table: The Crawford Hospitality Story

Jolie didn't start as a restaurant group. It started as one restaurant — Crawford & Son — and a belief that Raleigh deserved dining rooms built on real technique, honest ingredients, and the kind of hospitality that makes you want to come back next week.

Crawford & Son opened in 2016 and was named Best Restaurant of the Year. Then came Jolie, Brodeto, Crawford Brothers, and Bar Sousterre. Each restaurant is its own thing — different menus, different energy. But they share a foundation: Scott Crawford's commitment to technique, a front-of-house culture that treats hospitality as a craft, and the belief that restaurants are at their best when they feel like an extension of home.
