A Family Table: The Crawford Hospitality Story

January 20, 2025

Jolie didn't start as a restaurant group. It started as one restaurant — Crawford & Son — and a belief that Raleigh deserved dining rooms built on real technique, honest ingredients, and the kind of hospitality that makes you want to come back next week.

Crawford & Son opened in 2016 and was named Best Restaurant of the Year. Three years later, Jolie arrived next door — a French bistro inspired by a family trip to Paris and named after our daughter. Then came Brodeto, an Italian seafood restaurant rooted in Adriatic tradition. Then Crawford Brothers, a classic steakhouse at Fenton in Cary. Then Bar Sousterre, an intimate wine bar that operates on its own terms.

Each restaurant is its own thing. Different menus, different energy, different reasons to walk through the door. But they share a foundation — Chef Scott Crawford's commitment to technique, a front-of-house culture that treats hospitality as a craft, and the belief that restaurants are at their best when they feel like an extension of home.

That's the thread that connects all of it. We're a family that opened restaurants because we love feeding people. Everything else follows from that.
